How to File a Claim — Sample Process Guide
This document is a synthetic, educational example describing a generic health insurance claims process. It does not represent any real insurance company's actual procedures.
Step 1: Receive Services
Visit an in-network or out-of-network provider for covered medical services. Be sure to bring your member ID card to each appointment.
Step 2: Provider Submits the Claim
In most cases, your healthcare provider will submit the claim directly to the insurance plan on your behalf. This typically happens within a few business days of your visit.
Step 3: Claim Review
The plan reviews the claim to verify eligibility, coverage, and medical necessity. Claims are generally processed within 10 to 30 business days, depending on complexity.
Step 4: Explanation of Benefits (EOB)
Once processed, the member receives an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) showing the billed amount, the plan's allowed amount, what the plan paid, and the member's remaining responsibility.
Step 5: Claim Status
Claims are marked as Approved, Denied, or Pending. Approved claims are paid according to the plan's coverage terms. Denied claims include a reason code and instructions for filing an appeal. Pending claims may require additional documentation from the member or provider.
Step 6: Filing an Appeal
If a claim is denied, members have 180 days from the date of the denial notice to file a written appeal. Appeals should include supporting documentation such as medical records or a letter from the treating physician.
